How much do you know about popular rock bands?

AC/DC

AC/DC was formed in 1973 by Scottish-Australian brothers Malcolm and Angus Young. AC/DC is not only the most successful band to ever come out of Australia, it is also one of the most successful bands of all time.

AC/DC started out with:

Vocalist, Bon Scott

Lead Guitarist, Angus Young

Rythm Guitarist, Malcolm Young

Bassist, Cliff Williams

Drummer, Phil Rudd

Highway to Hell

The "Highway to Hell" is actually a very real place. It was given as a nickname to the Canning Highway in Australia. It runs from Fremantle (Where Bon Scott lived) and ends at a bar called The Raffles.

There is a large reflection of the road itself in the lyrics. As Canning Highway gets closer to the pub, there is a steep decline in the road: "No stop sign... speed limits... nobody gonna slow me down."

Back in Black

Back in Black serves as a tribute song to Bon Scott. The band released it just five months after the tragic death of their lead singer.

The entire Back in Black album was proof that although Bon Scott was gone, AC/DC could continue on without him, and they will.

Shoot to Thrill

This song has a predominantely sexual meaning, it explores a time in the bands life where the members would often enjoy that "rock and roll life".

This song really just explores life how it was for the band, with lots of drugs and intercourse. They really were living the life they were talking about.
